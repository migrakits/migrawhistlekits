# Building Whistle Kits: Large Scale (1,000+ units)

For large-scale kit production, assembling materials efficiently and getting them into the hands of community organizers is key. Kit recipients assemble the materials into whistle baggies—small 4x6" baggies containing a whistle, lanyard, folded zine, and cut rights card.

## Making Your Own Parts

### Manufacturing Whistles

**3D Printing is the best option for large-scale production:**

Use professional-grade 3D printers that can produce whistles in-house with excellent reliability and flexibility.

**Recommended Equipment for Sustainable Production:**
- **Two Bambu Lab H2S with AMS** (~$1,499 each) - This is the ideal setup for sustainable, manageable production
  - Full plate of whistles takes ~11 hours to print
  - Only requires 2 plate swaps per day (morning and evening) - very easy to manage
  - Two printers running 24/7 can consistently produce 7 kits per week (2,520 whistles/week)
  - Can scale up by adding more printers as needed
  - Slicer settings for Bambu printers are available directly from the [MakerWorld whistle page](https://makerworld.com/en/models/2390471-minnesota-loud-dual-tone-flat-whistle-optimized)

**Budget Alternative: Bambu Lab P2S with AMS** (~$799 each)
- The P2S produces the same quality whistles at a lower price point
- Smaller build plate means ~3 plate swaps per day instead of 2
- Good option if budget is tight or you want to start smaller before investing in H2S printers
  
**Filament Costs:**
- **Bambu Lab filament**: $14 per 1kg refill roll
- **Whistles**: ~4 grams each
- **Weekly production**: 2,520 whistles = ~10 kg filament = ~$140/week
- **Monthly production**: 10,080 whistles = ~40 kg filament = ~$560/month

**Benefits of 3D Printing:**
- **Cost per whistle: Less than $0.10** (including electricity, plastic, and printer depreciation)
- **Customization**: Produce the Minnesota Flag Whistle with proper colors and design
- **Flexibility**: Can quickly pivot to produce other supplies (eye washers, etc.) as community needs change
- **Control**: Full control over supply chain and quality
- **Speed**: Fast production turnaround for urgent needs
- **No MOQs**: No minimum order quantities from external suppliers

**Workflow:**
1. **Get whistle design files**: Download the Minnesota Flag Whistle design from:
   - [Printables.com](https://www.printables.com/model/1596509-minnesota-flag-4mm-loud-dual-tone-flat-whistle-opt)
   - [MakerWorld](https://makerworld.com/en/models/2390471-minnesota-loud-dual-tone-flat-whistle-optimized)
2. Load plastic filament and configure multi-color printing on AMS
3. Print whistles in batches (printers can run 24/7)
4. Quality check and set aside for kit assembly
5. Adjust colors/designs as needed for different regions or campaigns (the design can be modified to clamp any ribbon width)

### Manufacturing or Sourcing Lanyards

**Ribbon Lanyards with Printed Text (Recommended)**

The best option at large scale is to produce custom ribbon lanyards in-house with printed text and messaging.

**Equipment:**
- **RS103D Ribbon Printer** (~$300) with **Cutter attachment** (~$250) from [Ronsu](https://www.ronsutech.com/rs103d-satin-ribbon-printer-machine/): Industrial-grade ribbon printer with optional automatic cutter
- **Ribbon rolls** (200-meter rolls): Comes in various colors and materials
- **Carbon transfer film**: For print quality

**Process:**
1. Design ribbon graphics with text like "Protect Each Other," organization name, phone numbers, resources, etc.
2. Load 200-meter ribbon roll into printer with carbon transfer film
3. Print and automatically cut ribbons to appropriate lengths (3-foot ribbons or custom length as needed)
4. The printer's optional cutter attachment automatically cuts ribbons after each print - fully automated
5. Modified 3D printed whistle design clamps onto ribbon ends to create lanyard

**Benefits:**
- **Cost: $0.04 or less per lanyard** (including ribbon, printing, electricity, and equipment depreciation)
- **Customization**: Print different messages, phone numbers, QR codes, or organization info on each ribbon
- **Automation**: Optional cutter attachment automatically cuts ribbons - no manual cutting required
- **Quality**: Professional printed appearance makes kits look organized and intentional
- **Messaging**: Each lanyard is a communication tool, not just a functional strap
- **Durability**: Ribbon is durable and washable
- **Efficiency**: 200-meter rolls mean very little downtime reloading ribbon

**Integration with 3D Printed Whistles:**
- The updated 3D whistle design includes clamping mechanisms on both ends
- Ribbon threads through clamps and crimps or ties at the ends
- Creates a complete, professional whistle-and-lanyard assembly

### Printing in-House

**Zines & Cards with Epson C800 Printers (Recommended)**

Use professional Epson C800 color printers for all printing needs.

**Equipment:**
- **Epson C800 Color Printer** (from [Your Printer Toner Store](https://yourprintertonerstore.com/epson_workforce_pro_ep-c800_workgroup_color_printer_c11ck21201/)): one printer easily keeps up with two H2S 3D printers
- Standard office supplies: paper, cardstock, etc.

**Benefits:**
- **Lowest electricity consumption** compared to laser printers
- **Lowest ink costs** - color printing costs approximately $0.03 per page
- **Significantly cleaner** than laser printers - no dust, toner particles, or heat issues
- **Easy maintenance** - simple supplies, no complex procedures
- **High volume capacity** - reliable for thousands of prints per day
- **Professional quality** - crisp, vibrant colors for zines and cards

**Printing Costs & Supplies:**
- **8-page journal fold zine** (single sheet printed and folded): ~$0.03 per zine (ink only)
- **Rights card**: ~$0.03 per card (ink only, cut from standard pages)
- **Hammermill Copy Paper**: 5,000 pages cost $50-70 from brick-and-mortar or online retailers (~$0.01-0.014 per page)
- **Total printing cost per page**: ~$0.04-0.044 (ink + paper)

**Workflow:**
1. Design the 8-page journal fold zine in standard software (InDesign, Canva, etc.)
2. Print single sheets on Epson C800(s) - each sheet becomes one 8-page zine when folded
3. Design rights card layouts to fit on standard pages
4. Print, cut, and fold as needed
5. Simple maintenance with readily available supplies

## Large-Scale Production Workflow

### Step 1: Secure Funding & Materials

**Startup Costs (for sustainable weekly production of 7 kits)**

*Equipment (one-time investment):*
- **Two Bambu Lab H2S Printers with AMS**: ~$3,000 ($1,499 each)
- **RS103D Ribbon Printer**: ~$300 + cutter attachment ~$250
- **One Epson C800 Color Printer**: $600-1,200 (one printer easily keeps up with two 3D printers)
- **Supplies**: filament storage, nozzles, build plates, ribbon, carbon film, printer supplies, etc.: $300-500
- **One FedEx Scale**: $75-150 (for shipping kits)

**Total Equipment Startup: $4,525-5,400**

*Weekly Operating Costs (per 7 kits = 2,520 whistles):*
- Whistles (4g × 2,520 = ~10kg filament @ $14/kg): ~$140
- Lanyards (ribbon + printing): ~$100
- Zines (8-page fold, 2,520 × $0.042): ~$106
- Rights Cards (2,520 × $0.035): ~$88
- Baggies: ~$50
- Paper/Tape/Labels: ~$20
- **Weekly Materials: ~$504**

*Monthly Operating Costs:*
- Materials (~$504 × 4.3 weeks): ~$2,167
- Electricity, maintenance, misc: ~$200-300
- **Total Monthly Operating: ~$2,367-2,467**

**Cost per kit: ~$83** (materials only)

*At this scale, equipment investment is modest (~$5k) and weekly production is highly manageable. Two H2S printers running 24/7 with just two plate swaps per day produce consistent output. Equipment pays for itself quickly, especially if you charge a small fee per kit to cover costs.*

**Funding strategies for this scale:**
- Community fundraisers ($5k goal)
- Small grants from immigrant rights organizations
- Donations from foundations supporting community organizing
- Small fees to partner organizations ($10-25 per kit)
- Shared investment with partner organizations

### Step 2: Set Up Production Space

**Facility needs:**
- **500-800 sq ft dedicated space**: Sufficient for two H2S printers and supporting infrastructure
- **3D Printer Area**: Dedicated, climate-controlled space for two H2S printers running 24/7 with good ventilation
- **Ribbon Printer Area**: Space for RS103D ribbon printer with cutter, ribbon storage, and carbon film inventory
- **Printing Area**: Space for one Epson C800 color printer and paper storage
- **Climate control**: Essential - maintains optimal conditions for printer reliability and filament storage
- **Work tables**: Small assembly table for kit packing (can be done by 2-3 people)
- **Storage shelving**: For paper, baggies, ribbon materials, and finished kits
- **Shipping/staging area**: Small area for FedEx scale and kit box staging

**Typical space**: 500-800 sq ft accommodates all equipment with room to move and organized material storage

**Staffing:** 
- Minimal ongoing staffing - mostly printer management (plate swaps morning/evening)
- 2-3 people for kit assembly day (1-2 hours per week)
- Could be managed by a core team of volunteers

### Step 3: Build Assembly Infrastructure

**Kit packing workflow:**

Your infrastructure focuses on packing raw materials into kits for distribution to community organizers. Each kit contains unfolded zines, uncut rights cards, whistles (with lanyards attached), baggies, and assembly instructions. Recipients use these materials to assemble and distribute whistle baggies.

1. **Material prep station** - Organize bulk materials into batches
2. **Whistle QA** - Inspect and test whistles
3. **Lanyard attachment** - Attach lanyards to whistles
4. **Card sorting** - Organize cards by type/language
5. **Kit assembly station** - Pack bundles with unfolded zines, cards, whistles+lanyards, baggies, and instructions
6. **Quality check** - Verify each kit has all components
7. **Packing** - Pack complete kits into boxes with clear assembly instructions
8. **Inventory** - Track and log finished kits

### Step 4: Recruit & Organize Volunteers

**Kit packing needs:**
- 20-30 people can pack 400-600 kits (each producing 360 whistle baggies) in 4-6 hours
- Schedule multiple packing days for large orders
- Training: 30 minutes on kit packing procedures and quality checks
- Stations rotate every 30-45 minutes to prevent fatigue

**Ongoing operation:**
- Designate roles: Production manager, QA lead, logistics coordinator
- Create training materials so new volunteers can quickly pitch in
- Build a volunteer email list for callouts when packing days are scheduled

### Step 5: Manage Quality & Inventory

**Quality control system:**
- Spot-check completed kits at seal station
- Final QA station does 100% verification
- Track defect rate and address issues immediately
- Document batches with date/volunteer crew for traceability

**Inventory management:**
- Track raw materials: whistles, lanyards, zines, cards on hand
- Monitor production pace vs. supply levels
- Reorder materials 4-6 weeks before depletion
- Log finished kits by batch with date and volunteer crew

### Step 6: Scaling Up Production

**Equipment Ratio for Efficiency:**
- **One Epson C800 printer can easily keep up with two H2S 3D printers**
- This ratio ensures printing never becomes a bottleneck
- As you scale, add printers in pairs: 2 more H2S + 1 more C800

**Scaling Examples:**
- **Current setup** (7 kits/week): 2 H2S + 1 C800 + 1 RS103D
- **14 kits/week**: 4 H2S + 2 C800 + 1-2 RS103D
- **21 kits/week**: 6 H2S + 3 C800 + 2 RS103D
- Equipment costs stay proportional while maintaining efficiency

**Space & Staffing Scaling:**
- Each additional pair of H2S printers requires ~100-150 sq ft
- Kit assembly time scales linearly - 2-3 people can handle 7+ kits per day
- Minimal additional staffing required for significant production increases

### Step 7: Distribution at Scale - Build Visible Community Protection

The success of whistle kits is measured not by how many are produced, but by **how many whistles are actually worn visibly** in neighborhoods. Visible whistles create a powerful culture of community protection.

**Local distribution:**
- Partner with 10+ community organizations for simultaneous distribution
- Host multiple kit distribution events across the city/region
- **Create a campaign around WEARING whistles** - emphasize visibility and mutual protection
- Train distribution partners to promote whistle visibility and encourage ongoing wearing
- Provide promotional materials (posters, social media content) emphasizing that visible whistles = community power
- Track how many people are actively wearing whistles, not just kits distributed

**Regional/national distribution:**
- Ship kits to allied organizations in other cities
- **Use FedEx OneRate for best pricing** - a cube-shaped FedEx Large Box fits a complete kit (360 whistles) and ships 2nd day for ~$30
- Coordinate with immigrant rights networks
- Create a simple order form on website
- Share best practices for promoting whistle visibility and cultural adoption
- Negotiate volume shipping rates if shipping many boxes regularly

**Feedback loop:**
- Collect feedback from recipients about kit contents and assembly process
- Learn what works and what could be improved
- Track how many whistle baggies recipients produce and distribute
- Document impact stories and share back with community
