# "Form a Crowd, Stay Loud"

We protect each other by bringing **awareness and visibility** to ICE operations in our neighborhoods. The whistles are an immediate alert system that is **"loud, recognizable, and impossible to ignore."** They **"turn silence into community action."**

**When you wear a whistle visibly on a lanyard, you signal to your neighbors: "I will protect you. You are not alone. We stand together."** This visibility is powerful. It creates a culture of mutual protection. It shows that an entire community is mobilized and ready.

> These quotes are from the "Form a Crowd, Stay Loud" zine from the Pilsen Arts Community House in Chicago.

The whistle system has been used effectively in **Los Angeles, Chicago, Minneapolis, St. Paul, Portland,** and cities across the nation. They are a **non-violent means for communities to hold ICE publicly accountable** for their actions.

---

## What are Whistle Kits?

A **Whistle Kit** is a collection of bulk supplies that community organizers use to assemble and distribute **whistle baggies** to their neighbors.

> **Kit** = bulk supplies for an organizer (360 whistles, lanyards, zines, rights cards, baggies, and assembly instructions)
>
> **Whistle Baggie** = the finished package for a neighbor (1 whistle + lanyard + folded zine + rights card, sealed in a 4x6" baggie)

Kit makers source or produce the components and pack them into kits. **Kit recipients** do the hands-on work — folding zines, cutting rights cards, attaching lanyards, assembling baggies — then distribute them so **everyone can wear and see whistles in your community**.

The zine teaches communities how to use the whistles together as a signal of mutual protection. **When whistles are visible and worn, they become a powerful symbol of community power.**

### The Minnesota Flag Whistle

Minnesotans showed the nation how to respond. The **Minnesota Flag Whistle** was created and distributed during January and February 2026, at the height of ICE operations. It is now increasingly being distributed outside of Minnesota as a **symbol and tribute** to the example they set.

## How Can You Help?

There are many ways to contribute your time, resources, and social network to spread the whistle system in your neighborhood and across the nation.

### 1. **Wear a Whistle - Build Visible Community**

Get a whistle—any whistle will do. **Wear it visibly on your lanyard every day.** The visibility is powerful:
- Shows your neighbors you're ready to protect and be protected
- Creates a visible culture of mutual aid and community solidarity  
- Signals to ICE that an entire community is watching and mobilized
- Encourages others to get whistles and wear them too
- Builds a chain reaction of protection across your neighborhood

**Every visible whistle is a statement: "We are here. We are watching. We protect each other."**

### 2. **Carry & Share**
Grab extra whistles and distribute them along with printed copies of the "Form a Crowd, Stay Loud" zine. Hand them out to people who ask about yours.

### 3. **Donate Supplies**
Buy whistles and lanyards, print zines and rights cards, and **donate to local organizations** or community members building kits or distributing whistles.

### 4. **Build Whistle Kits (Small Scale)**

For quantities up to ~1,000 kits, purchase pre-made whistles and lanyards, then order printed zines and rights cards. Assemble kits, then share them with volunteers and organizations who will use them to produce and distribute whistle baggies to their neighbors.

[**See detailed small-scale instructions →**](small-scale-kits.md)

### 5. **Build Whistle Kits (Large Scale)**

**For serious scale**, set up production infrastructure to assemble and distribute thousands of kits. Kit recipients handle the work of assembling whistle baggies, allowing you to amplify impact exponentially.

[**See detailed large-scale instructions →**](large-scale-kits.md)

## Building Your Kits

Whistle kits contain all the materials needed to assemble whistle baggies. Each whistle baggie contains:

- **Whistle** (with lanyard attached)
- **Folded zine** - "Form a Crowd, Stay Loud"
- **Rights card** (Tarjeta de Derechos in Español)
- **4x6" baggie** - sealed with all contents inside

To assemble kits, you'll need:

- **Whistles** - Order in bulk online (Amazon: ~$0.60/unit at 1,000+ quantity)
- **Lanyards** - Available from craft/outdoor suppliers (Amazon: ~$0.10/unit at 1,000+ quantity)
- **Zines** - Print locally or order from print shop
- **Rights Cards** - Print locally on cardstock (use provided design)
- **Baggies** - 4x6" ziplock bags

See the kit instructions document for detailed assembly and packaging steps.
